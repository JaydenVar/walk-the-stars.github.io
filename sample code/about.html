What is Medical Telecommunications?

Also referred to as telehealth or telemedicine in healthcare, medical telecommunications is healthcare delivered remotely using various forms of communication. This allows patients to consult a physician even when they are unable to meet them in person. This form of healthcare not only is convenient for patients looking for flexibility but can also be used by rural communities that may not have easy access to physical healthcare services.

With the current circumstances surrounding the pandemic, the need for telecommunication in healthcare has dramatically increased. To minimize human interaction, many healthcare settings have opted to offer telehealth as an alternative to physically visiting a doctor. This ensures that patients receive the care they need, without high risk of transmitting or receiving disease through contact with others.

Popular forms of consultations are the internet, video conferencing, or even asynchronous communication between the patient and their physician, such as text messaging.
